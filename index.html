Beneficial Microbes

Microbes that positively affect human health include:

Gut Microbiota

- Aid digestion and nutrient absorption.
- Produce vitamins (e.g., B12, K).
- Strengthen immune function.
- Protect against pathogens.

Skin Microbiota

- Protect against harmful microbes.
- Support skin barrier function.
- Aid wound healing.
- Influence immune responses.

Oral Microbiota

- Maintain oral health.
- Prevent tooth decay.
- Protect against oral diseases.
- Aid digestion (saliva enzymes).

Harmful Microbes

Microbes that negatively impact human health include:

Pathogenic Bacteria

- Cause infections (e.g., tuberculosis, strep throat).
- Produce toxins damaging to host cells.
- Trigger immune responses leading to inflammation.
- Contribute to chronic diseases.

Viruses

- Cause viral infections (e.g., flu, COVID-19).
- Hijack host cell machinery to replicate.
- Trigger immune responses.
- Can lead to chronic infections.

Fungi

- Cause fungal infections (e.g., athlete's foot, ringworm).
- Produce allergens.
- Can be opportunistic pathogens.
- Affect respiratory health.

Probiotics

Live beneficial bacteria that provide health benefits when consumed. Examples include:

Lactobacillus

- Common in yogurt and fermented foods.
- Helps in digestion.
- Supports immune function.
- Reduces symptoms of IBS.

Bifidobacterium

- Found in the intestines.
- Aids in digestion.
- Supports immune function.
- Reduces symptoms of IBS.

Vaccines

Vaccines are biological preparations that provide immunity against specific diseases. Examples include:

Polio Vaccine

- Uses inactivated poliovirus.
- Prevents poliomyelitis.
- Administered orally or via injection.
- Highly effective.

MMR Vaccine

- Protects against measles, mumps, and rubella.
- Uses live attenuated viruses.
- Administered via injection.
- Routine childhood vaccine.

Bioremediation

Use of microbes to clean up environmental contaminants. Techniques include:

Ex Situ Bioremediation

- Contaminants removed from site.
- Treated in a controlled environment.
- Examples: biopiles, land farming.
- Effective for controlled remediation.

In Situ Bioremediation

- Treatment at the contamination site.
- Less disruptive to the environment.
- Examples: bioventing, biosparging.
- Suitable for groundwater and soil.
